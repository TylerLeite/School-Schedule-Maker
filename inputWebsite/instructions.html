How to use the scheduler

The Pierrepont Scheduler™ is a brand new technology that reduces the amount of time of scheduling all of the classes for students by up to 7000% and guarantees no embarassing situations where two classes are scheduled to take place in the same room at the same time. The Pierrepont Scheduler™ is specifically designed to make your life as easy as possible, but the method of input may take some explanation. Don't worry, though, I'm here to help.

Step 1

The scheduler works by taking a list of classes and constructing lists of students, teachers, rooms, and courses based on the input. It does all of this automatically so that you, the user, only have to worry about putting people in the right class and typing carefully. It is important to note that due to the dynamic nature of the scheduler, there is no way for it to tell if you've mistyped a student or course name. As such, we at CS3 inc. recommend that you triple check all names before hitting submit. Changing a name by hand can be a tedious process.

For step 1, go to the "Courses" tab and simply input the name of a course, how often it meets, the teacher's name, all students' names, and all rooms that the class can take place in. NOTE: You must, of course, use full names of people and courses. The scheduler will treat a teacher named Jack Smith the same as a student named Jack Smith.

Step 2

The second step of the scheduling process is to tell the scheduler about teacher restrictions. If a teacher cannot be in school on a day or at a time, you must use the form in the "Teachers" tab to indicate so. In order to toggle all spaces, check the box in the upper left corner. To toggle a whole day at a time, simply check the box next to that day. You can also check indiviudual times by clicking it's respective box.

Step 3

The third and final step is to simply run the scheduler.jar file. This requires Java. Your computer should already have it installed, but if not, you can download it from: http://www.java.com/en/download/index.jsp The output will be in the form of html files which can be clicked on to open them in your default web browser. You can use this information to make schedules in whatever format you prefer, it is just a middleman of sorts to give you all the information you need to make a nice looking schedule.
